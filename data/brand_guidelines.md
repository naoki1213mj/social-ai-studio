## Brand Voice & Tone

### Overall Tone
- **Professional yet approachable**: We're experts who don't take ourselves too seriously
- **Data-driven**: Back claims with numbers and evidence
- **Forward-looking**: Focus on what's next, not just what is
- **Inclusive**: Technology is for everyone, avoid jargon when possible

### Platform-Specific Tone

#### LinkedIn
- **Tone**: Thought leadership, professional insights
- **Style**: Longer-form, data-backed, industry analysis
- **Audience**: CTOs, Engineering Managers, Enterprise Decision Makers
- **Do**: Share industry insights, company milestones, technical deep-dives
- **Don't**: Be overly casual, use memes, or post clickbait

#### X (Twitter)
- **Tone**: Developer-friendly, witty, real-time
- **Style**: Concise, thread-friendly, emoji-enhanced
- **Audience**: Developers, DevRel community, Tech Enthusiasts
- **Do**: Share quick tips, engage in tech conversations, use relevant emoji
- **Don't**: Be corporate-speak, ignore trending conversations

#### Instagram
- **Tone**: Creative, behind-the-scenes, human
- **Style**: Visual storytelling, carousel posts, Stories-friendly
- **Audience**: Tech talent (recruiting), developer community, general tech enthusiasts
- **Do**: Show team culture, office moments, event highlights
- **Don't**: Post text-heavy content without visuals, be overly technical

### Content Dos and Don'ts

#### Do:
- Lead with value â€” what does the reader gain?
- Include a clear call-to-action (CTA)
- Use data and statistics to support claims
- Tag relevant people, companies, and communities
- Respond to comments within 2 hours during business hours
- Cross-reference content across platforms with different angles

#### Don't:
- Post the same content verbatim across all platforms
- Use more than 5 hashtags per post (except Instagram: up to 10)
- Share unverified technical claims or benchmarks
- Engage in controversial political or social commentary
- Use competitor names negatively
- Post without proofreading
